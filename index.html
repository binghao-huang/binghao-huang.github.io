Current Projects

Neural Combinatorial Optimization on Graphs

ARCLab, UCSD
Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) is a popular example of NP-hard combinatorial optimization problems that require exponential computational time to solve to optimality. Many classical algorithms based on human-designed heuristics have been proposed to compute near-optimal solutions to TSP and its variants within an acceptable time constraint. In this project, we attempt to surpass the classical algorithms by combining the recent progress in neural modeling of sequence, graphs, and Deep Reinforcement Learning, to automatically learn to solve these problems end-to-end.

Neural Motion Planning with Guided Collision Avoidance

ARCLab, UCSD
Motion Planning Networks are not guaranteed to output valid states, so they rely on a ground-truth collision checker to filter out invalid states, which can be computationally expensive. Furthermore, they can only feed-forward the neural network again when a sampled state is invalid. We seek to accelerate neural planning with an ML-based fast differentiable collision detector.
